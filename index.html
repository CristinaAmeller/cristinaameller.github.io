About Me!

Introduction

Hi and welcome! My name is Cristina and I live in Miami, Florida. I like everything from playing music, art direction, painting, and handbuilt ceramics.

Expressing myself through art has always been second nature to me. I found my own community through these hobbies and a career that was a perfect fit for me.

In this blog, I'll explain how each of my passions became apart of my life and why they are so important to me.

Music

Ever since I can remember, I have been playing the drums. My dad had bought me a mini drum kit and I used it as much as I possibly could. By the time I was in the third grade

I got an actual drumset so that my dad and I can play our favorite songs. I practiced as I got older and picked up the guitar. I a lot of the friends I currently have are through

our similar taste in music. This is also how I made two of my bands. We release music and play shows with other bands while also building a community through our music. Writing my

experiences and sharing them with other people who can also resonate with it means a lot.

Art Direction

I'm currently studying creative advertising at the Univeristy of Miami. I always knew I was going to UM because my mom had worked there since I was in elementary school.

Before I got accepted I was at FIU studying biology on a pre-med track. I went through STEM classes and even shawdowed doctors while they performed C-Sections. By the time the

pandemic hit, I knew it wasnt for me. Once I looked through the program options at UM I automatically knew that it was fate. They had graphic design, art direction and typography courses.

I paired this major with a studio art minor. Studying studio art made me try other art forms that helped me unlock the depths of my identity.

Studio Art

I mainily indulged in ceramics and painting courses when I picked them for my minor. Ceramics helped me learn about patience and really understanding to perfect techniques.

I've made boxes, a vase, spirit vessels and pots for flowers. I've also learned my painting style through the classes i've taken. Realism is very interesting but I think abstract art

can really push you to be creative. Once I have a vision for it I'm focused on executing it.
